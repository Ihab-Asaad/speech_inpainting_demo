Audio samples from "Self-Supervised Learning of Speech Representations - Application to Speech Inpainting"

Abstract: This paper introduces a deep-learning-based approach that utilizes self-supervised learning techniques for unconditioned speech inpainting. While existing approaches have achieved impressive results, their efficacy remains limited for long masks (>200 ms). The methodology assumes prior knowledge of the corrupted timesteps that needs to be inpainted and involves fine-tuning a pretrained HuBERT large model on a single GPU to predict the corresponding Mel-Spectrogram frames generated by a simple k-means model of 100 clusters. To achieve the final restoration of the speech audio signal in the time-domain, we utilize HiFi-GAN - a neural speech vocoding and excels at transforming mel-spectrograms back into high-quality speech waveforms while preserving the speakerâ€™s identity. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in restoring corrupted speech timesteps ranging up to 400 ms in duration on the LJ Speech dataset. In addition, the performance surpasses a simple baseline, which employs a straightforward linear interpolation of the corrupted mel-spectrogram frames. The comparison between the proposed approach and the baseline is conducted using three metrics, namely Average Root Mean Square Error for evaluating the predicted mel-spectrogram frames, as well as Word Prediction Error, and the Short-Time Objective Intelligibility metric for evaluating the intelligibility of the inpainted speech signal.

Our implementation is available in the github repository.
